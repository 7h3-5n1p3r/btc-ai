1. INTRODUCTION TO BTR TOURISM

The Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR), located in Assam, India, is one of the most naturally rich and culturally diverse regions in Northeast India. With dense forests, national parks, rivers, and tribal heritage, BTR has immense tourism potential.

Tourism in BTR is primarily eco-tourism, wildlife tourism, and cultural tourism.

====================================================================

- Conservation of biodiversity

4. MANAS NATIONAL PARK (UNESCO WORLD HERITAGE SITE)

Location: Baksa & Chirang districts

Key Features:
- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Project Tiger Reserve
- Biosphere Reserve

Biodiversity:
- Bengal Tiger
- Indian Elephant
- One-horned Rhinoceros
- Golden Langur (rare species)

Landscape:
- Grasslands
- Forests
- Riverine ecosystems

Activities:
- Jungle safari
- Bird watching
- River rafting

Significance:
Manas is one of India’s most important conservation areas and attracts both national and international tourists.

====================================================================

5. RAIMONA NATIONAL PARK

Location: Kokrajhar district

Established: 2021

Key Features:
- Assam’s 6th National Park
- Known for Golden Langur habitat
- Part of transboundary conservation landscape (India-Bhutan)

Flora & Fauna:
- Dense forest ecosystems
- Rare and endangered species

Tourism Potential:
- Eco-tourism
- Jungle trekking
- Wildlife photography

====================================================================

6. CHAKRASHILA WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

Location: Kokrajhar & Dhubri border

Key Features:
- Second protected habitat of Golden Langur
- Hilly terrain and scenic beauty

Activities:
- Trekking
- Wildlife observation
- Nature photography

====================================================================

7. KAKOIJANA RESERVE FOREST
