1. INTRODUCTION

The Bodoland Territorial Council (BTC) is an autonomous governing body established under the Sixth Schedule of the Constitution of India. It administers the Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR) in Assam, which is home to a diverse population including Bodo, Adivasi, and other indigenous communities.

BTC represents one of India’s most important experiments in decentralized governance, granting legislative, administrative, and financial powers to a regional body.

====================================================================

2. HISTORICAL EVOLUTION

The demand for Bodoland has deep historical roots. It began as a movement for identity, autonomy, and protection of tribal rights.

- 1960s: Plains Tribal Council of Assam (PTCA) raises autonomy demand
- 1987: All Bodo Students Union (ABSU) intensifies movement
- 1993: BAC (Bodoland Autonomous Council) formed
- 2003: BTC established
- 2020: New Bodo Peace Accord strengthens BTC

====================================================================

3. POLITICAL MOVEMENTS AND STRUGGLES

The Bodoland movement saw:
- Mass protests
- Civil disobedience
- Armed insurgency (by groups like BLT, NDFB factions)
- Negotiations with central and state governments

This movement shaped modern BTC governance.
