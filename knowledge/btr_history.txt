1. INTRODUCTION

The Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR) represents a unique socio-political and cultural entity in Assam, India. Its history is deeply rooted in the identity, struggles, and aspirations of the Bodo people, one of the largest indigenous communities of Northeast India.

====================================================================

2. ORIGIN AND HISTORY OF THE BODO PEOPLE

The Bodo people belong to the Tibeto-Burman linguistic family and are considered among the earliest settlers of the Brahmaputra Valley.

Key Characteristics:
- Indigenous tribal community
- Strong connection with nature
- Rich oral traditions

====================================================================

3. EARLY SETTLEMENTS

The Bodos historically inhabited:
- Brahmaputra Valley
- Foothills of Bhutan and Arunachal Pradesh

They practiced:
- Agriculture
- Forest-based livelihoods

====================================================================

4. ETHNIC IDENTITY

The Bodo identity evolved through:
- Language preservation
- Cultural practices
- Community-based living

====================================================================

5. COLONIAL PERIOD

British colonial rule impacted:
- Land ownership systems
- Tribal autonomy
- Administrative boundaries

Result:
- Gradual marginalization of tribal communities

====================================================================

6. POST-INDEPENDENCE DEVELOPMENTS

After 1947:
- Integration into Assam state
- Growing demand for political recognition

====================================================================

7. RISE OF BODO MOVEMENTS

The movement for autonomy intensified due to:
- Identity concerns
- Economic backwardness
- Political marginalization

====================================================================

8. PTCA (PLAINS TRIBAL COUNCIL OF ASSAM)

Formed in the 1960s:
- Demanded separate state “Udayachal”
- Early political mobilization

====================================================================

9. ABSU MOVEMENT

All Bodo Students Union (ABSU) led:
- Mass protests
- Strong political campaigns

Slogan:
- “Divide Assam 50-50”

====================================================================

10. ARMED MOVEMENTS

Militant groups emerged:
- Bodo Liberation Tigers (BLT)
- National Democratic Front of Bodoland (NDFB)

Purpose:
- Achieve autonomy through armed struggle

====================================================================

11. 1993 BAC AGREEMENT

Agreement between:
- Government of India
- Government of Assam
- Bodo leaders

Outcome:
- Formation of Bodoland Autonomous Council (BAC)

====================================================================

12. LIMITATIONS OF BAC

- No clear territorial boundaries
- Limited administrative powers
- Weak implementation

Result:
- Continued dissatisfaction

====================================================================

13. DEMAND FOR GREATER AUTONOMY

Failure of BAC led to:
- Renewed protests
- Demand for stronger governance

====================================================================

14. 2003 BODOLAND ACCORD

Signed on 10 February 2003

Parties:
- Government of India
- Government of Assam
- Bodo Liberation Tigers (BLT)

Outcome:
- Formation of Bodoland Territorial Council (BTC)
- Inclusion under Sixth Schedule

====================================================================
